1. Development

Pladipus was originally developed at the CompOmics group at Ghent University, Belgium (part of VIB) as part of a pipeline for large scale reprocessing of public proteomics data from the PRIDE database. Since the initial developments, Pladipus has been enhanced to have many capabilities which can be applied to many computationally intense problems that can be "embarrassingly parallelised". Since the initial prototype, Pladipus has become a true cluster management system and has been continuously operated at the CompOmics group.

Examples of applied usages for the Pladipus Framework applications include:
  • Extraction of both precursor and fragment ion mass accuracies for all projects in PRIDE
  • Generation of a database of theoretical peptides using MS2PIP
  • Reprocessing of all human projects in PRIDE against UniProtKB

2. Requirements
