Pladipus - bringing cluster management to the masses

Pladipus was originally developed by the CompOmics group at Ghent University, Belgium (part of VIB) as part of a pipeline for the large scale reprocessing of public proteomics data from the PRIDE database.

It has since been extended into a generic cluster management system that can be applied to various computationally intense problems that can be parallelized.

Examples of applied usage of Pladipus includes:
  • Extraction of precursor and fragment ion mass accuracies for all projects in PRIDE
  • Generation of a database of theoretical peptides using MS2PIP
  • Reprocessing of all human projects in PRIDE against UniProtKB

Requirements: i) access to a MySQL-server, ii) access to an ActiveMQ server, and iii) a central repository to store results/input data.
